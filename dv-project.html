The impact of government policies on Covid19 pandemic

author: Garnier Raphael, Guerin Clement
date: January 14th 2022


This work has been made for our project of data visualization. Its goal is to show the impact of the various policies each european government implemented during the Covid-19 pandemic. Please note that our data stops at January 14th 2022.
You can select one European country to highlight. By default France is selected. You can also select a government policy. By default face covering is selected. Finally you can select the factor you want to display. It can be the number of daily new cases, deaths, the population in public parks, at work, in public transport or in grocery shops. By default, daily new cases is selected.

Legend and explainations

This graph shows the impact of various levels on the number of covid-related death (per milion) in for a period of 30 days.
This graph is separated in multiple sub-graphs, each of them showing a different level of the policy (from lower to higher). For each sub-graph you see the evolution of the number of covid-related deaths (per milion) in percentage relative to the first day of application of that policy level. Each graph shows the data for the first time this very policy level has been implemented.

The horizontal line indicates the amount of deaths on day 1: if the orange line goes under it means that the policy is effective.

WARNING : Even if the policy level is increasing from left to right on the graph, the policy levels may not have been implemented in chronological order
