The impact of government policies on Covid19 pandemic

author: Garnier Raphael, Guerin Clement
date: January 14th 2022


This work has been made for our project of data visualization. Its goal is to show the impact of the various policies government implement during the Covid19 pandemic on the later one. Please note that our data stop at January 14th 2022.
You can select one European country to highlight. By default France is selected. You can also select a government policy. By default face covering is selected. Finally you can select the factor you want to display. It can be the number of daily new cases, deaths, the population in public parks, at work, in public transport or in grocery shops. By default, daily new cases is selected.

Legend and explainations

This graph represent the impact of various level on the number of new covid cases in for a period of 30 days. How to read this graph? This graph is separated in multiple sub-graphs, each one of them represent a different level of the policy (from lower to higher). For each sub-graph you see the evolution of new covid cases in percentage relatively to the first day of application of that policy.

The horizontal line indicate the amount of death on day 1, if the orange line goes under it means that the policy is effective.

WARNING : Ascending policy level might not be implemented chronologically
